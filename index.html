01 — Cast

Six agents,
one continuous thought.

Each agent has a single job and a single voice. They hand work to one another the way a small studio passes a manuscript across desks.

  1. 01

    Planner

    Reads the topic. Extracts keywords. Drafts the research questions and the outline that everything else hangs on.

    — intent
  2. 02

    Researcher

    Reaches into ArXiv, pulls down papers and abstracts, and quietly builds the knowledge base the rest of the system will speak from.

    — sourcing
  3. 03

    Retriever (RAG)

    Embeds everything with MiniLM, stores the vectors in FAISS, and answers the harder question: which paragraphs actually matter?

    — memory
  4. 04

    Writer

    Drafts the intro, literature review, methods comparison, challenges and conclusion — all grounded in the retrieved evidence.

    — voice
  5. 05

    Editor

    Tightens clarity, holds the tone steady, restructures where the logic has wandered. The quiet adult in the room.

    — craft
  6. 06

    Compositor

    Stitches the sections, writes the executive summary, and exports a finished research document, citations intact.

    — finish

03 — Process

From a sentence,
to a survey.

  1. 01

    You write a topic.

    Anything from “agentic memory architectures” to “FAISS vs. HNSW for legal corpora”.

  2. 02

    The Planner sketches a plan.

    It distils keywords, drafts research questions, and proposes the spine of the survey.

  3. 03

    The Researcher pulls real papers.

    Live calls to the ArXiv API. Abstracts and metadata flow into a working knowledge base.

  4. 04

    FAISS does semantic search.

    Everything is embedded, indexed, and retrieved by meaning rather than keyword.

  5. 05

    The Writer drafts. The Editor refines.

    Sections appear: introduction, literature review, methods, challenges, conclusion — grounded in retrieved evidence.

  6. 06

    The Compositor delivers.

    Final assembly. Executive summary on top. A clean, exportable research document at the bottom.
